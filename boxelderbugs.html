Boxelder Bugs

Recognizing and managing a common winter nuisance in Michigan homes.

What Are Boxelder Bugs?

Boxelder bugs are black-and-red insects commonly found around boxelder, maple, and ash trees. They do not bite or cause structural damage, but they often gather in large numbers on sunny walls and enter homes during cooler months.

Behavior & Characteristics

  • Most active in fall and winter
  • Enter homes through small exterior gaps
  • Produce a strong odor when crushed
  • Cluster in warm, sunny exterior areas

Damage

Boxelder bugs pose little structural or health risk but are major nuisance pests. Indoors, they stain surfaces with excrement and release an unpleasant odor when crushed. Outdoors, they may slightly distort leaves or fruit on boxelder, maple, or ash trees but rarely cause serious damage. They don’t bite or spread disease, but large fall invasions into homes make them particularly bothersome.

How to Prevent or Remove Boxelder Bugs

1. Seal Cracks Around Your Home

Use silicone caulk to seal window frames, siding gaps, door edges, and utility openings.

2. Remove Bugs by Vacuuming

Vacuum instead of crushing to avoid stains and odors.

3. Apply Mild Soap Spray Outdoors

A mix of dish soap and water helps eliminate clusters safely.
